The Problem

The Semantic Gap

⚠ Current Reality (Trust Protocol)

India's FASTag system is blind. When an RFID tag broadcasts "I am a Car," the barrier opens — even if a 12-wheel commercial truck is carrying it. The digital identity $ID_{tag}$ does not match the physical reality $ID_{phys}$. This causes crores in annual revenue leakage.

✓ Our Proposal (Truth Protocol)

We give the toll barrier a pair of eyes. A live camera feed runs through a computer vision engine in parallel with the RFID scan. The system independently determines the vehicle's actual class and audits the tag's claim before the barrier moves.

Verification Flow
01

The Claim (RFID)

Tag broadcasts an ID. Database returns the registered vehicle class — e.g., "Light Motor Vehicle."

02

The Reality (Vision)

Camera feeds live video to our CV engine. Shape, size, and edge structure determine the actual physical class.

03

The Audit (Handshake)

Both streams are compared. A match opens the barrier; a mismatch triggers a silent alert for inspection.

Computer Vision Pipeline

How the Engine Works

Edge Finder — Gradient Calculation
Detect where pixel intensity changes sharply. Sharp changes = vehicle boundaries: sides, roof lines, windshields.
Horizontal & Vertical Gradients
$$G_x = \text{PixelRight} - \text{PixelLeft}$$ $$G_y = \text{PixelUp} - \text{PixelDown}$$

A large $|G_x|$ indicates a strong vertical edge — like the tall flat side of a truck. A large $|G_y|$ indicates a strong horizontal edge — like a car's low roofline.

Edge Strength & Direction
Convert raw gradients into two meaningful descriptors: how strong the edge is, and which direction it runs.
Magnitude (Strength)
$$m = \sqrt{G_x^2 + G_y^2}$$
Orientation (Direction)
$$\theta = \arctan\!\left(\frac{G_y}{G_x}\right)$$

Trucks produce many strong, near-vertical edges (box geometry). Cars produce softer, more curved edges with less dominant vertical structure. This difference drives the classifier.

Lighting Robustness — Normalisation
Toll cameras face harsh variation: direct sun, cloud cover, headlights, shadows. Normalisation removes global brightness as a confound.
L2 Normalisation
$$v_{norm} = \frac{v}{\sqrt{\|v\|^2}}$$

Dividing by the vector's energy preserves the pattern of features while eliminating lighting-scale effects:

Sunny → [100, 200, 100] Cloudy → [10, 20, 10] Normalised → [0.4, 0.8, 0.4]
Decision Maker — Support Vector Machine
Learn a maximum-margin boundary between CAR and TRUCK feature spaces from labelled training examples.
SVM Decision Function
$$f(x) = w \cdot x + b$$

Where $x$ is the normalised feature vector, $w$ is the learned weight vector, and $b$ is the bias. The SVM learns $w$ and $b$ to maximise the margin between classes.

$f(x) > 0$
→ TRUCK
$f(x) < 0$
→ CAR
